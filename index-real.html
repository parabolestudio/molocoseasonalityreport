All I Want for Christmas (is ROAS)

Insider insights from Moloco to help you make the most of the 2025 holidays

Report by

As the seasoned marketer knows, the holiday season provides a dual narrative: the threat of increased competition and the prospect of discovering more valuable, more engaged users. With the right planning, the right insights, and the right execution, you can achieve a decisive edge and take advantage of a hectic but opportunistic period.

Moloco analyzed internal numbers and integrated external data to understand the full opportunity for app marketers over the holiday season.

The seasonality timeline

The holiday season can be hectic for both shoppers and marketers, but success is within reach for those who adapt. By aligning ad strategies with the three key periods that shape Q4, marketers can drive stronger performance and capture more value during the most competitive time of year.

User Behavior Trends

Gaming and consumer apps follow distinct seasonal patterns. Gaming sees pronounced Q5 lift driven by new device activations and increased leisure time, while consumer apps peak earlier during Black Friday through Christmas as purchase intent concentrates around gift-giving and year-end spending. Understanding these differences is critical for optimal budget allocation.

The Opportunity Gap

The most powerful insights emerge when you layer user behavior against advertiser activity. Use the chart below to dynamically compare metrics—time spent vs. CPM, revenue vs. bid requests, or session duration vs. ROAS—and uncover the gaps where consumer demand and advertiser competition diverge.

Take Q5 as an example (the period immediately following Christmas Day through mid-January), time spent in apps surges as users enjoy more leisure time at home, yet bid requests drop as advertisers pull back. The result? Highly engaged users available at lower costs. Meanwhile, in-app revenue often rises as consumers spend holiday gift cards, while ROAS improves due to reduced auction pressure. These disconnects—where user value is high but competition is low—represent your biggest efficiency opportunities.
